# Kuala Lumpur Food Guide -- Hidden Gems & Local Favorites

> Focus: Curry, beef, ramen/noodles, cheap & authentic, no seafood, no fine dining, no tourist traps.
> Base: Near KLCC / Jalan Kia Peng (Novotel KL City Centre area)

---

## 1. BEST CURRY SPOTS

### Mamak Stalls & Nasi Kandar

**Mohd Yaseen Nasi Kandar**
- Location: 351 Jalan Tuanku Abdul Rahman, Chow Kit
- Order: Ayam kicap, ayam madu, daging kicap with kuah campur (mixed curry flood-style over rice)
- Price: Cheap (around RM10-15 per person)
- Why: 24-hour operation. Curry is cooked fresh throughout the day. Massive queue at dinner -- that is how you know it is legit. No-frills plastic chairs, fluorescent lights, pure local energy. The late Tan Sri Syed Mokhtar used to eat here.
- Crowd: Packed at dinner (7-9pm). Best at late night (11pm-2am) when queues die down.

**Nasi Kandar Kudu Bin Abdul**
- Location: Jalan Tuanku Abdul Rahman, Chow Kit (near Mohd Yaseen)
- Order: Ayam bawang (onion chicken), daging kicap, sirap bandung to drink
- Price: Cheap (RM10-15)
- Why: Operating since 1969. Was the favorite spot of legendary Malaysian actor P. Ramlee. The curries are rich, heavy on spice, and the crowd is 100% local. Zero English signage.
- Crowd: Busy at lunch and dinner. Go mid-afternoon for a calmer experience.

**Syed Bistro**
- Location: Bangsar
- Order: Nasi kandar with kuah campur banjir (flood curry), pick chicken/beef/lamb plus freshly sauteed veggies
- Price: Cheap (RM12-18)
- Why: Neighborhood mamak that locals swear has the best nasi kandar in KL. Not a chain, not fancy -- just consistently excellent curries with proper depth of flavor.
- Crowd: Steady flow. Best at lunch when curries are freshest.

**NZ Curry House**
- Location: Near KLCC (Jalan Ampang area)
- Order: Nasi briyani kambing (lamb biryani), maggie goreng mamak, roti canai with dhal
- Price: Cheap-Moderate (RM10-20)
- Why: Walking distance from your hotel. Aromatic curries, good roti, and a genuine mamak atmosphere. Popular with nearby office workers.
- Crowd: Lunch rush is intense. Evenings are more relaxed.

**Nasi Kandar Pelita (KLCC Branch)**
- Location: Jalan Ampang, near KLCC (walkable from Novotel)
- Order: Nasi kandar with ayam goreng berempah, daging masak merah, kuah campur
- Price: Cheap (RM15-20 for two including drinks)
- Why: 24-hour operation. The go-to mamak for the KLCC area. It is a chain, but each outlet cooks independently and the KLCC branch is solid. This is where locals go after midnight when nothing else is open.
- Crowd: Always busy. 2-4am is the quietest window.

**TG's Nasi Kandar**
- Location: Golden Triangle, Bukit Bintang
- Order: Nasi kandar, roti canai, cheese naan
- Price: Cheap (RM10-15)
- Why: 24 hours, right in the Golden Triangle. Perfect for late-night curry cravings. Affordable and unpretentious.
- Crowd: Late-night crowd 11pm-3am is the real experience.

**Nasi Kandar Gani Maju**
- Location: Bukit Jalil (further out, but worth the trip)
- Order: Ayam bawang, kuah campur banjir (flood-style mixed curry)
- Price: Cheap (RM10-15)
- Why: Established 1970. Authentic Penang-style nasi kandar in KL. Rich, layered curries that hit different from KL-style. Generous portions.
- Crowd: Meal times are hectic. Go slightly off-peak.

### Indian Curry (South & North Indian)

**Vishal Food & Catering**
- Location: 22 Jalan Scott, Brickfields (Little India)
- Order: Banana leaf rice (RM6.80) with mutton curry, chicken varuval, rasam
- Price: Dirt cheap (RM7-12)
- Why: Quiet street near the Indian temple. Banana leaf rice with 3 side dishes for under RM7. The curries are legit South Indian home-cooking. No pretense whatsoever.
- Crowd: Lunch is peak. Mornings and late lunch are calm.

**Lawanya Food Corner**
- Location: 8 Jalan Scott, Brickfields
- Order: Banana leaf rice, mutton curry, chicken varuval, thosai
- Price: Dirt cheap (meals under RM5 possible)
- Why: Run by the same family for 30+ years. Home-cooked curries displayed in clay pots. The mutton curry is exceptional. The thosai is considered the best in KL. This is as authentic as it gets.
- Crowd: Locals pack it at lunch. Arrive by 11:30am.

**Moorthy's at Money's Corner**
- Location: Brickfields (Little India)
- Order: Mixed rice (chap fan style -- point at what you want), Indian fried chicken, fish head curry (skip the fish, get the curry with other protein)
- Price: Cheap (RM8-15)
- Why: Hawker stall inside a coffee shop. The fried chicken is deep-fried to order -- crispy, fragrant, and addictive. The curries rotate daily.
- Crowd: Lunch only, gets busy fast.

**Mr Naan & Mr Idly**
- Location: Brickfields
- Order: Indian buffet (both North and South Indian vegetarian)
- Price: Cheap (RM15 all-you-can-eat)
- Why: RM15 buffet with proper Indian curries, naan, idly, dosa, and more. Incredible value. Vegetarian-focused but the curry flavors are outstanding.
- Crowd: Lunch crowds. Go early.

**Restaurants along Jalan Masjid India**
- Location: Jalan Masjid India (near Chow Kit)
- Order: Nasi briyani, various curries, roti varieties
- Price: Cheap
- Why: An entire street of Indian Muslim restaurants. No English menus. Just point and eat. The concentration of curry here is unmatched in KL.
- Crowd: Lunch is the main event. Evening for a calmer vibe.

### Malaysian Curry

**Madam Chiam Curry Noodle House**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Curry mee (curry noodles), dry chilli pan mee
- Price: Cheap (RM8-12)
- Why: Always swarming with locals. The curry mee broth is rich with coconut milk and spice. Many say the dry chilli pan mee is actually the hidden star. A proper old-school noodle house.
- Crowd: Meal times are packed. Mid-afternoon is quieter.

## 2. BEST BEEF DISHES

### Beef Noodles

**Soong Kee Beef Ball Noodles**
- Location: 86 Jalan Tun H.S. Lee (opposite UOB Bank), nearest LRT: Pasar Seni
- Order: Dry beef noodles with minced beef sauce + side bowl of clear beef broth with handmade beef balls
- Price: Cheap (RM8-12)
- Why: Operating since 1945. Founded by Siew Koy Soong, inspired by Hakka beef noodles from Tai Po, Hong Kong. The springy handmade beef balls are legendary. The minced beef-pork sauce over egg noodles is umami heaven. You will hear slurping before you see the stall -- follow the sound.
- Crowd: Perpetually crowded. Queue is part of the experience. Go right at opening.
- Note: Has branches across Klang Valley, but the original is the one to visit.

**Sin Kiew Yee Shin Kee Beef Noodles**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Beef noodle soup, dry beef noodles
- Price: Cheap (RM8-12)
- Why: Another beloved KL beef noodle institution. Rich, beefy broth that has been simmered for hours. The beef slices are tender and the noodles have perfect chew.
- Crowd: Lunch is busiest.

**Mee Tarik Restoran (Hand-Pulled Noodles)**
- Location: Jalan Sultan, Chinatown
- Order: Hand-pulled noodles in beef broth with tender beef slices
- Price: Cheap (RM10-15)
- Why: "Mee tarik" means hand-pulled noodles. Watch them stretch the dough to order. The beef broth is rich and the noodles have that irreplaceable hand-pulled texture -- chewy, irregular, and satisfying. Open until 2am, making it a perfect late-night beef noodle spot.
- Crowd: Dinner and late night are busiest. Great supper option.

### Beef Rendang

**Nasi Lemak Kak Ilah**
- Location: Kuchai Lama
- Order: Nasi lemak with beef rendang, turmeric beef, sweet spicy sambal
- Price: Cheap (RM8-15)
- Why: Hidden gem in a residential area. The beef rendang is slow-cooked until deeply caramelized and falling apart. The sambal is sweet-spicy perfection. This is kampung (village) cooking done right.
- Crowd: Lunch is peak. Can sell out early for popular items.

**Nasi Lemak Wanjo**
- Location: Kampung Baru (Jalan Raja Muda Musa)
- Order: Nasi lemak with beef rendang, sambal, fried chicken
- Price: Cheap (RM8-12)
- Why: Established 1963. The most famous nasi lemak in Kampung Baru. The rendang is rich and the sambal is legendary. A large metal shed of a restaurant -- zero pretense, maximum flavor.
- Crowd: Always packed. Dinner and late evening are the scene.

**Nasi Lemak Antarabangsa**
- Location: Kampung Baru
- Order: Nasi lemak with beef rendang
- Price: Cheap (RM8-12)
- Why: Established 1973. The eternal rival of Wanjo. Some locals swear this one is better. Try both and decide for yourself. Same authentic Kampung Baru vibe.
- Crowd: Dinner crowd is strong.

### Beef Satay

**Satay Zulkifli (Satay Zul)**
- Location: 5 Jalan Besar, Kampung Baru
- Order: Beef satay, lamb satay (RM1.30/stick), with peanut sauce and nasi impit (compressed rice)
- Price: Cheap (RM15-25 for a solid meal of 10-15 sticks)
- Why: The lamb satay is marinated in a secret spice blend and grilled over charcoal to tender perfection. The peanut sauce is thick and creamy. Kampung Baru at night with satay smoke in the air is a quintessential KL experience.
- Crowd: Opens 3pm, busiest 7-10pm. Go early evening.
- Hours: 3:00 PM - 11:00 PM daily

**Sate Kajang Haji Samuri**
- Location: Multiple outlets across KL (20+ branches)
- Order: Beef satay, chicken satay, lamb satay -- order 20+ sticks minimum
- Price: Cheap-Moderate (RM1-1.50 per stick)
- Why: The most famous satay name in Malaysia. The peanut sauce alone is worth the visit -- rich, creamy, with just the right spice kick. Beef satay is marinated overnight and grilled over coconut shell charcoal.
- Crowd: Always busy at dinner. Late evening is better.

**Maarof Satay Kampung Baru**
- Location: Kampung Baru
- Order: Beef and chicken satay with rice cakes
- Price: Cheap
- Why: Serving satay for generations. The skewers are grilled darker than usual, giving a smoky char that is deeply satisfying. The fragrant rice cakes are the perfect vehicle for the peanut sauce.
- Crowd: Evening only. Peak around 8pm.

**Satay Capitol 1951**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Beef satay, chicken satay
- Price: Cheap
- Why: Over 70 years of satay-making. Grilled slightly darker with herbs worked into the marinade. Has a depth of flavor that newer places cannot replicate.
- Crowd: Evening operation.

## 3. BEST RAMEN & NOODLE SPOTS

### Japanese Ramen

**Kanbe Ramen**
- Location: Central KL (multiple outlets)
- Order: Aka ramen (fermented chili paste), or Kokuou (rich tonkotsu). The broth is 48-hour simmered pork bone.
- Price: Moderate (RM25-35)
- Why: Seven distinctive serving styles. The 48-hour pork bone broth is legitimately on par with good ramen in Japan. The Aka with fermented chili paste will satisfy any spice lover. Small, cramped space -- feels like a Tokyo ramen-ya.
- Crowd: Lunch and dinner queues. Go at 2-3pm for shortest wait.

**Minamo Ramen**
- Location: KL
- Order: Chicken broth ramen (boiled for precisely 5 hours in pH-specified water)
- Price: Moderate (RM25-35)
- Why: Run by the same chef behind Soranoiro in Tokyo (Bib Gourmand rated). The precision is real -- pH-controlled water, exact timing. This is as close to Tokyo-quality ramen as you will find in KL.
- Crowd: Moderate. Ramen purists know about this place.

**Maruki Ramen (Halal)**
- Location: KL
- Order: Beef-based ramen broth with braised beef and crispy fish skin topping
- Price: Moderate (RM20-30)
- Why: Malaysia's first halal-certified beef-based ramen broth. The braised beef is tender and the broth is rich. A unique KL twist on ramen that you will not find in Japan.
- Crowd: Moderate.

### Local Noodle Dishes (The Real Stars)

**Restoran Kin Kin (Original Chilli Pan Mee)**
- Location: 40 Jalan Dewan Sultan Sulaiman, Kampung Baru
- Order: Dry chilli pan mee with fried egg on top -- add as much dried chilli flakes as you dare
- Price: Cheap (RM7-10)
- Why: THE birthplace of chilli pan mee. Invented here by owner Tan Kok Hong in 1985. Flat hand-torn noodles, minced pork, fried shallots, a poached egg, and a mountain of dried chilli flakes. The texture contrast is addictive. This dish changed KL's food scene.
- Crowd: Perpetually packed. Opens 7:30am. Go early morning or late afternoon (closes 9pm weekdays, 4pm weekends).
- Hours: 7:30am-9pm (Mon-Fri), 7:30am-4pm (Sat-Sun)

**Restoran Super Kitchen Chilli Pan Mee**
- Location: Directly opposite Kin Kin on Jalan Dewan Sultan Sulaiman (also 8 outlets across KL)
- Order: Chilli pan mee -- they put a full container of dried chilli flakes on every table
- Price: Cheap (RM7-10)
- Why: The eternal rival of Kin Kin. Some locals prefer Super Kitchen's version. The unlimited chilli flakes on the table is a power move. Try both, pick your side.
- Crowd: Same as Kin Kin -- packed at meal times.

**Madam Chiam Curry Noodle House**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Curry mee, dry chilli pan mee
- Price: Cheap (RM8-12)
- Why: The curry mee is rich with coconut milk and the noodles swim in a deeply spiced broth. But insiders know the dry chilli pan mee here rivals the famous spots. A proper locals-only noodle house.
- Crowd: Meal times are packed.

**Hometown Ban Mee**
- Location: Sentul
- Order: Pan mee in boiled anchovy soup, curry mee, dry chilli pan mee
- Price: Cheap (RM7-10)
- Why: Freshly made pan mee with a deeply savory anchovy broth. The noodles are hand-torn to order. The curry mee variant is also worth trying. Located in a residential area -- purely local crowd.
- Crowd: Lunch is busiest.

**Kim Lian Kee Hokkien Mee**
- Location: Original at Petaling Street; also at Lot 10 Hutong
- Order: Hokkien mee (thick yellow noodles, dark soy sauce, fierce wok hei)
- Price: Cheap (RM8-12)
- Why: KL Hokkien mee is nothing like the Singaporean version. Thick noodles tossed in dark soy over extreme heat, with pork lard cracklings and cabbage. The wok hei (breath of the wok) is the defining flavor. Kim Lian Kee is the standard bearer.
- Crowd: Lunch at the original. Lot 10 Hutong is more accessible.

**Pudu Wai Sek Kai Noodle Stalls**
- Location: Pudu (the entire Wai Sek Kai street)
- Order: Wander and try -- chee cheong fun, Hokkien mee, various noodle stalls
- Price: Dirt cheap (RM5-10)
- Why: Pudu is the real street food heart of KL. Every corner is a different noodle variation. No English, no tourists, just locals eating.
- Crowd: Morning to early afternoon is the main window.

## 4. HAWKER CENTERS & FOOD COURTS

### Top Tier (Authentic, Local-Heavy)

**ICC Pudu (Imbi Market)**
- Location: ICC Pudu building, Pudu
- Must-try stalls:
  - Ah Fook Chee Cheong Fun & Yong Taufu (curry gravy worth the wait)
  - Mee Halia (ginger wine noodles, RM9)
  - Ann Nasi Lemak (nasi lemak with pork skin curry, from RM9)
  - IMBI Kueh Bakul (triple sticky rice cake, RM1.80)
  - Traditional Popiah stall (RM2.80)
- Price: Dirt cheap (RM5-12 per dish)
- Why: Over 100 hawker stalls on the ground floor. This is a working wet market with a food court attached. The crowd is entirely local -- aunties, uncles, market workers. No tourist has ever accidentally wandered in here. Food starts at 5:30am and many stalls sell out by noon.
- Crowd: Go before 10am for full selection. CLOSED ON MONDAYS.
- Hours: 5:30am - 2pm (or until sold out)

**Pudu Wai Sek Kai**
- Location: Pudu district
- Must-try stalls: Grilled chicken wings, chee cheong fun with sweet sauce, fried radish cake, various noodle stalls
- Price: Dirt cheap (RM3-8)
- Why: An entire street where every stall is a temptation. This is where KL locals go when they say "let's go eat" -- not Jalan Alor. Raw, authentic, zero concessions to tourists.
- Crowd: Morning to early afternoon. Some stalls open at night.

**Melayu Selera Food Street (Pudu)**
- Location: Within Pudu area
- Must-try: Roti canai, nasi lemak, smoky satay skewers grilled over coals
- Price: Dirt cheap
- Why: Completely different vibe from the Chinese hawker stalls nearby. Malay food cooked over open flames. The satay smoke alone will pull you in.
- Crowd: Evening is when the satay fires get lit.

**Kampung Baru Saturday Night Market**
- Location: Jalan Raja Alang, Kampung Baru (LRT: Kampung Baru station)
- Must-try: Satay, roti john, ayam percik, nasi lemak, mee goreng mamak, kuih-muih (Malay sweets)
- Price: Dirt cheap (RM3-10 per item)
- Why: The last Malay enclave in central KL. Saturday night market is a cultural experience as much as a food one. Traditional wooden houses in the shadow of the Petronas Towers. The ayam percik (flame-grilled spiced chicken) is not to be missed.
- Crowd: Saturdays only, 6pm-11pm. Gets very busy by 8pm. Go at 6pm for the full experience.

### Solid Options (Mix of Local and Some Tourists)

**Lot 10 Hutong**
- Location: Basement of Lot 10 Shopping Mall, Bukit Bintang
- Must-try stalls:
  - Kim Lian Kee Hokkien Mee (legendary dark soy noodles)
  - Soong Kee Beef Ball Noodles (the branch, not the original)
  - Various curry and noodle stalls
- Price: Cheap-Moderate (RM10-18)
- Why: Founded in 2009 to preserve Malaysian culinary heritage by gathering famous hawker stalls under one roof. Yes, it is in a mall. Yes, some tourists come. But the stalls are the actual famous operators -- Kim Lian Kee, Soong Kee, etc. Air-conditioned, which is a legitimate advantage in KL heat. Good fallback when you do not want to hunt.
- Crowd: Lunch is packed. Go at 3pm or after 8pm.

**Jalan Imbi Open-Air Hawker Area**
- Location: Walk to the Ritz Carlton, turn right at Jalan Imbi -- open-air carpark across the street functions as a hawker centre at night
- Must-try: Various stalls (changes regularly)
- Price: Cheap
- Why: A hidden hawker centre that most tourists walk right past. It is literally an open-air carpark that transforms at night. The stalls are local and rotating.
- Crowd: Night only. Best after 7pm.

## 5. CAFES WITH CHARACTER

### Heritage & Hidden Gems

**Merchant's Lane**
- Location: Petaling Street, Chinatown (look for the old green door)
- Order: Chicken rendang sandwich, soft-shell crab pasta with curry sauce, coffee
- Price: Moderate (RM15-30 per dish)
- Why: Hidden behind a nondescript green door on Petaling Street. Upstairs in an old pre-war building. Wooden elements, colorful furnishings, and a sense of discovery. Malaysian flavors fused with Western cafe fare. One of the spots that revived Chinatown's food scene.
- Crowd: Weekends are packed. Weekday afternoons are ideal.

**Chocha Foodstore**
- Location: 156 Jalan Petaling, Chinatown
- Order: Modern Malaysian dishes with locally sourced ingredients, natural wine
- Price: Moderate (RM20-40)
- Why: Housed in a century-old building that was formerly the Mah Lian Hotel (which was actually a brothel). The heritage architecture is stunning -- exposed brick, original tiles, and a serene garden area. Modern Malaysian cuisine that respects tradition. The building itself tells a story.
- Crowd: Evenings and weekends busiest. Weekday lunch is peaceful.

**Broom**
- Location: Bukit Bintang
- Order: Coffee, pastries
- Price: Moderate (RM12-20)
- Why: Nestled inside a two-storey heritage home. Original floor tiles and windows have been retained. The age of the building seeps through every detail. Feels like drinking coffee in someone's pre-war grandparent's house.
- Crowd: Manageable most times.

### Specialty Coffee (Unique Spaces)

**Feeka Coffee Roasters**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Any single-origin pour-over, brunch items
- Price: Moderate (RM12-25)
- Why: Raw yet warm aesthetic. Has been a top brunch and coffee spot for over a decade. Not a chain, not a trend-chaser -- just consistently excellent coffee in a space with genuine character.
- Crowd: Weekend brunch is popular. Weekday mornings are calm.

**Curio Coffee Roaster**
- Location: Central KL (climb a spiral staircase to find it)
- Order: "Aerocano" (iced Americano textured with steam wand -- their signature)
- Price: Moderate (RM12-18)
- Why: Accessible only via a spiral staircase. Mosaic floors, uncluttered design. The Aerocano is famous among KL coffee drinkers. Feels like a secret you discovered.
- Crowd: Coffee crowd peaks mid-morning.

**Curio Dark**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Espresso-based drinks
- Price: Moderate (RM12-18)
- Why: All-black aesthetic. Blink-and-you-miss-it entrance. The polar opposite of Curio Coffee Roaster in vibe but same quality. For those who like their coffee spots moody and hidden.
- Crowd: Small space, rarely overcrowded.

**Fade by SB**
- Location: KL
- Order: Pour-over coffee
- Price: Moderate (RM12-18)
- Why: Japanese aesthetic with simple wood paneling and corner windows. Every cup and saucer is from a thrift store -- mismatched and unpretentious. The furniture does not match either. The effect is deeply calming and personal.
- Crowd: Quiet most of the time. The way it should be.

**Piu Piu Piu**
- Location: ZhongShan building (artistic/creative hub)
- Order: Cold brew (their claim to fame)
- Price: Moderate (RM10-15)
- Why: Only 270 sqft. Tiny espresso bar in an artistic building filled with creatives. The cold brew is famous among KL coffee insiders. This is not a place you stumble upon -- you have to know about it.
- Crowd: Tiny space means it feels full with 5 people.

### Rooftop & Views

**Cotta**
- Location: Level 6 rooftop, Verve Shops, Mont Kiara
- Order: Ricotta-based dishes (their entire concept revolves around ricotta), coffee
- Price: Moderate (RM20-35)
- Why: A lush, plant-filled rooftop hidden on top of a Mont Kiara building. Ricotta-led dining concept that is genuinely unique -- not just a gimmick. The space is picture-perfect and breezy.
- Crowd: Weekends are popular. Weekday afternoons are serene.

**curio L5**
- Location: UR-MU museum rooftop (inside the museum building)
- Order: Coffee
- Price: Moderate (RM12-18)
- Why: WEEKENDS ONLY. A rooftop coffee shop inside a museum, with patio views of KL's skyline. Under-the-radar even for locals. The definition of "hidden."
- Crowd: Small, weekend-only. Go early.

## 6. LATE NIGHT FOOD (After 9PM)

### 24-Hour Operations

**Nasi Kandar Pelita (multiple locations)**
- Nearest to you: Jalan Ampang (walkable from KLCC)
- Order: Nasi kandar, roti canai, teh tarik
- Why: The 2am mamak experience is a Malaysian rite of passage. Order roti canai and teh tarik at 3am and watch the roti guy flip dough like a performer.
- Best time: 11pm-3am for the authentic late-night mamak vibe.

**Mohd Yaseen Nasi Kandar**
- Location: Chow Kit
- Order: Full nasi kandar spread
- Why: 24 hours. The late-night crowd is a different energy from the dinner rush. Taxi drivers, night-shift workers, insomniacs. The curries keep coming fresh.

**ABC Bistro Cafe**
- Location: Brickfields
- Order: Nasi kandar, roti canai, maggi goreng
- Why: 24/7 operation in Little India. Solid mamak food at any hour.

### Late-Night Specialists

**Mee Tarik Restoran**
- Location: Jalan Sultan, Chinatown
- Order: Hand-pulled beef noodles
- Price: Cheap (RM10-15)
- Why: Open until 2am. Watch them pull noodles at midnight. The beef broth is rich and warming. Perfect post-exploration supper.
- Hours: Open daily, serves until 2am

**Tapak Urban Street Dining**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Browse multiple street food vendors
- Price: Cheap-Moderate (varies by vendor)
- Why: Open-air street food market operating 4pm-3am daily. Multiple vendors, good atmosphere, outdoor seating. A curated street food experience without the chaos.
- Hours: 4pm - 3am daily

**Devi's Corner**
- Location: Bangsar
- Order: Roti canai, nasi lemak, naan, tandoori chicken
- Price: Cheap (RM8-15)
- Why: The Bangsar late-night institution. Usually packed at midnight. This is where people go to "lepak" (hang out) after everything else closes. The roti canai is reliable and the vibe is pure local.
- Crowd: Midnight-2am is peak social hour.

**Valentine Roti**
- Location: Central KL
- Order: Roti canai, capati, tosai -- they specialize in all things roti
- Price: Cheap (RM3-8)
- Why: Open 10am-3am (Mon-Sat, closed Sunday). Wide selection of roti varieties. Said to serve some of the best roti canai in KL. The roti guy's technique is mesmerizing.
- Hours: 10am - 3am (Mon-Sat)

**Yan Yan A One (Late-Night Dim Sum)**
- Location: KL
- Order: Xiao long bao, har gao, siu mai
- Price: Cheap-Moderate (RM15-25)
- Why: Dim sum until 2am. One of the few places in KL where you can get proper dim sum at midnight. The xiao long bao is the star.
- Hours: Open until 2am

### Late-Night Areas to Wander

**Jalan Alor (Bukit Bintang)**
- Note: Yes, it is touristy. But it is also genuinely lively at night. Walk through for the atmosphere but eat at the less prominent stalls toward the back of the street.
- Best after: 9pm-midnight
- Skip: The stalls with English menus and photos out front. Go deeper.

**Kampung Baru (evenings)**
- Multiple satay stalls, nasi lemak spots, and Malay food vendors light up in the evening. Walk along Jalan Raja Muda Musa.
- Best: 7pm-11pm

## 7. NEAR KLCC / JALAN KIA PENG (Walking Distance from Novotel)

### Within 5-10 Minutes Walk

**Nasi Kandar Pelita (Jalan Ampang)**
- Distance: ~5 min walk
- Order: Nasi kandar, roti canai, teh tarik
- Price: Cheap
- Why: Your closest 24-hour mamak. Reliable curries. Perfect for jet-lag eating at odd hours.
- Open: 24 hours

**NZ Curry House**
- Distance: ~5-7 min walk (Jalan Ampang area)
- Order: Nasi briyani kambing, roti canai, maggie goreng
- Price: Cheap-Moderate
- Why: Aromatic curries within easy walking distance. Popular with local office workers.

**Madam Kwan's (Suria KLCC)**
- Distance: ~10 min walk (inside KLCC mall)
- Order: Nasi lemak with beef rendang, curry laksa
- Price: Moderate (RM20-35)
- Why: Yes, it is in a mall. But this is where KL locals actually eat -- it is consistently packed with Malaysians, not tourists. The beef rendang is legitimately excellent. Good introduction to Malaysian flavors in a comfortable setting.
- Crowd: Lunch is always a queue. Go at 2:30pm.

**The LINC KL**
- Distance: ~10-12 min walk
- What: Collection of boutique cafes, dessert bars, artisanal coffee
- Price: Moderate
- Why: An artsy enclave with greenery and independent cafes. Not a chain in sight. Good for afternoon coffee and cake after exploring KLCC.

### Within 10-20 Minutes Walk

**Lot 10 Hutong (Bukit Bintang)**
- Distance: ~15-20 min walk (or 1 LRT stop)
- Order: Kim Lian Kee Hokkien Mee, Soong Kee Beef Noodles

## TIPS
- Download Grab app (Southeast Asia's Uber) -- rides are very cheap
- Most hawker stalls are cash only -- carry small bills (RM5, RM10)
- "Kuah campur banjir" means flood the rice with mixed curries -- always say this at nasi kandar
- Point-and-eat is perfectly acceptable at any hawker stall or banana leaf place
- Water is often not free at mamaks -- order teh tarik (pulled milk tea) or teh ais (iced tea) instead
- Avoid Monday at ICC Pudu (closed) and plan Kampung Baru night market for Saturday
