# Vignesh's Travel Persona

> Paste this into any AI chat when planning a trip. It gives full context on how I travel, what I love, and what to avoid suggesting.

---

## Who I Am

I'm an experienced independent traveler based in India. I plan all my trips myself, 2-3 months ahead, with detailed itineraries. I travel with my girlfriend, who shares the same interests, so there's no need to account for differing preferences. I don't speak any foreign languages but manage well with a phone translator app.

I'm physically fit (gym 4x/week), can handle 13+ km walking days comfortably, and have no mobility issues. I prepare my legs before trips with treadmill training and bring kinesiology tape and recovery tools.

---

## Travel Style

**Pace:** Fast. No rest days. I'm out by 9am and power through full days. I'll stay 3-4 nights in one place only if there's enough to fill the days or it's a good base for day trips.

**Independence:** I plan everything myself. No guided tours, no group experiences, no organized activities unless they're the only way to access something truly worth it.

**Accommodation:** Budget of roughly $100 USD/night. I'm open to local-style stays (ryokans in Japan, boutique guesthouses elsewhere) but don't need luxury. Just clean and functional.

**Transport:** I strongly prefer public transit, walking, and cycling. I have an International Driving Permit and will rent a car only if a destination is genuinely inaccessible otherwise.

**Planning approach:** When starting a new trip, I prefer an inspiring outline first (key regions, themes, rough route). Once the direction is confirmed, I move into detailed day-by-day planning. I appreciate interactive maps and visual route tools during the planning process.

---

## What I Love

**Hidden cultural sites over famous landmarks.** I'd rather discover a quiet, centuries-old neighborhood temple than fight crowds at the most famous one. I actively seek out places that most tourists skip. If something is "off the beaten path" and culturally rich, I'm interested.

**Peaceful traditional gardens.** One of my favorite travel discoveries. I love stroll-style gardens with beautiful landscaping, seasonal plants, and a calm atmosphere. Uncrowded is the key word.

**Hot springs and thermal baths.** Outdoor baths with scenic views (mountains especially) are peak travel for me. My best travel memory is an outdoor rotemburo overlooking mountains in Yufuin, Japan. I'm interested in "bath town hopping" wherever this exists.

**Goshuin collecting (Japan-specific).** I'm an active collector of temple and shrine seal stamps. I'll go out of my way for unique or rare ones, especially at small hidden temples. If planning a Japan trip, always factor this in.

**Easy multi-day cycling.** I loved cycling the Shimanami Kaido (2 days). I'm not a serious cyclist, so routes need to be accessible difficulty, but I'm very open to scenic cycling as part of a trip. E-bikes are fine.

**Moderate hiking.** I'll do day hikes gladly and up to 2-day hikes if the payoff is exceptional. Not interested in anything more strenuous or requiring camping.

**Classical/historical museums.** Castle museums, history museums, cultural exhibits. I'm open to contemporary art if it comes up naturally but won't seek it out.

**Pop culture (selectively).** I'm a Ghibli fan and a Pokemon fan. I enjoy theme parks and experiences tied to these specifically. Not interested in generic theme parks.

---

## Food Preferences

**Favorites:** Ramen (especially Hakata/tonkotsu style), curry, beef dishes, sushi. I gravitate toward hidden local gems and hole-in-the-wall spots rather than famous restaurants.

**Avoid:** Seafood broadly (it's a smell issue, not taste), with sushi being the one exception. I also don't seek out fine dining, Michelin restaurants, or expensive food experiences. I'm not a food snob, I just want something delicious and authentic.

**Neutral:** I enjoy coffee and alcohol casually if I encounter them, but I won't plan around specialty coffee shops, sake tastings, or brewery visits.

---

## What Doesn't Work For Me

**Crowds.** This is the single biggest factor that can ruin an experience. I've had otherwise great destinations completely undermined by overcrowding. Always be honest about crowd levels and suggest timing strategies or lesser-known alternatives.

**Festivals and events.** Too crowded, not my thing.

**Craft workshops.** Pottery, dyeing, cooking classes, etc. Not interested.

**Traditional performing arts.** Kabuki, noh, ballet, opera, etc. Not for me.

**Guided tours.** Unless it's the only way to access something exceptional.

**Generic theme parks.** I've done Disneyland once and won't revisit that type of experience.

**Evening activities (mostly).** I typically wind down after dinner. I haven't found evening activities that resonate, though I'm potentially open to things like night illuminations, quiet strolls through atmospheric neighborhoods, or a low-key local bar if the vibe is right.

**Remote islands or ultra-remote destinations.** The effort and cost usually don't justify the reward for the limited time I have.

**Adventure sports.** No camping, fishing, kayaking, diving, snorkeling, or similar.

## How to Recommend Things to Me

1. **Prioritize hidden gems over famous attractions.** If you suggest something popular, always pair it with a crowd-avoidance strategy or a lesser-known alternative.
2. **Be honest about crowd levels.** Don't sugarcoat it. Let me decide whether a crowded spot is worth my time.
3. **Mention if a car would significantly improve the experience.** I prefer not to drive, but I'll do it if the payoff is real.
4. **Suggest food spots that locals love.** Skip the tourist-trap restaurants and Michelin guides. Give me the ramen shop down an alley or the curry place with no English menu.
5. **Factor in my pace.** I can cover a lot of ground in a day. Don't under-schedule.
6. **Use maps and visual route planning** when presenting itineraries. I find these very helpful for reviewing and refining plans.
7. **Start with an outline, then go detailed.** Don't jump straight into a day-by-day itinerary unless I ask. Start with the big picture so I can steer the direction.
